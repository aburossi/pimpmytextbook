**1. Roll-Play Debate (Script + PowerPoint Recording)**  
Students collaboratively create a debate scenario with a short, coherent script and record a PowerPoint presentation with voiceover. 
B1-Competence:
- Present clear main arguments, each supported by at least one simple reason or example.
- Include a basic counterargument and address it with a concise response.
- Maintain a logical structure in the debate flow, assigning distinct roles to each speaker.
-  Locate at least one credible online source or factual piece of evidence to strengthen the debate points.
- Use AI tools to brainstorm arguments and formulate key talking points, then adapt them into your own words.

---

**2. Future Scenario (PowerPoint Report)**  
Students develop a structured future scenario related to a given topic, highlighting possible outcomes and presenting these in a PowerPoint report. 
B1-Competence:
- Describe at least two potential future developments and how they could arise.
- Provide simple justifications or reasons for each development, referencing basic cause-effect relationships.
- Present a clear, easy-to-follow PowerPoint structure with key points and short explanatory notes.
-  Find factual data or recent trends online to support each future development.
- Use AI to generate ideas for future scenarios and refine the phrasing, maintaining your personal style.

---

**3. Beginner’s Guide (PowerPoint Tutorial)**  
Students create a step-by-step tutorial to help newcomers understand a process or concept, demonstrated through a clear PowerPoint presentation. 
B1-Competence:
- Break the process into clear, sequential steps with short explanations for each.
- Provide at least one example or tip for each step, ensuring the guide feels practical.
- Use simple but correct language in slides and voiceover, keeping the tutorial cohesive.
-  Research common pitfalls or FAQs through relevant websites to ensure a helpful guide.
- Brainstorm guide structure or potential user questions with AI tools; incorporate these insights into the final tutorial.

---

**4. Historical Timeline (PowerPoint Presentation)**  
Students develop a chronological timeline of key events and present them in a concise PowerPoint. 
B1-Competence:
- List the main milestones in logical order, each with a short descriptor.
- Include at least one simple cause-effect link or short explanation for each milestone.
- Provide a cohesive PowerPoint layout with visuals supporting the timeline.
-  Use search engines to confirm key dates, figures, or background facts.
- Utilize AI suggestions for structuring the timeline or finding relevant historical details, then rephrase them in your own words.

---

**5. Ethical Dilemma (PowerPoint Storyboard)**  
Students illustrate a moral or ethical conflict through a brief storyboard, explaining different perspectives and a personal stance in PowerPoint format. 
B1-Competence:
- Outline at least two distinct perspectives on the ethical dilemma.
- Offer a personal stance, supported by one or two clear points.
- Use simple but coherent narrative structure in the storyboard (intro to dilemma, conflict, resolution).
-  Find brief examples or real-world parallels online to illustrate each perspective.
- Explore different ethical angles with AI brainstorming and refine the final storyboard text independently.

---

**6. Short Story (PowerPoint Episode)**  
Students create and present a concise story (3–5 minutes) through structured PowerPoint slides. 
B1-Competence:
- Develop a logical storyline with an introduction, a central conflict or main event, and a conclusion.
- Use slightly varied vocabulary and brief descriptive elements to make the story more vivid.
- Maintain clarity in narration, with slides and short spoken commentary aligning well.
-  Seek inspiration in basic story structures or relevant themes via online resources.
- Generate ideas for plot twists or character traits with AI, then adapt them to maintain personal creativity.

---

**7. Elevator Pitch (PowerPoint Presentation)**  
Students produce a concise, 1-minute pitch (±2 seconds) using at least five image slides, aiming to persuade an audience of an idea’s value. 
B1-Competence:
- Present a clear, simple value proposition, explaining why the idea is beneficial.
- Highlight at least one unique selling point or advantage, using straightforward comparisons if needed.
- Keep the pitch within 60 seconds (±2 seconds) while maintaining smooth delivery.
-  Find basic statistical data or examples online to support the pitch.
- Brainstorm concise slogans or taglines with AI, then adapt them to match the pitch style.

---

**8. Comparison Infographic (PowerPoint Presentation)**  
Students design an infographic-style presentation contrasting two approaches, explaining similarities, differences, and pros/cons. 
B1-Competence:
- Identify at least three straightforward comparison points (e.g., cost, speed, outcome).
- Provide short, clear pros and cons for each approach.
- Use simple comparative expressions (“whereas,” “in contrast,” “on the other hand”) in both slides and commentary.
-  Gather basic facts or metrics for each approach from reputable sources.
- Generate layout ideas for the infographic or short comparative phrases, ensuring final content is adapted into a personal style.

---

**9. Social-Media Reel (or PowerPoint Storyboard)**  
Students create a short, dynamic reel concept for social platforms, presented via PowerPoint storyboard with a brief voiceover. 
B1-Competence:
- Convey a central theme or message in under one minute of spoken text.
- Maintain a logical sequence of visuals or scenes, with transitions that are easy to follow.
- Use short, catchy phrases and straightforward vocabulary suitable for a wide audience.
-  Research trending reel formats or styles online to inspire the storyboard structure.
- Brainstorm creative visual ideas or key catchphrases using AI tools, then refine them for consistency.

---

**10. Personal Reflection (PowerPoint Video Diary)**  
Students reflect on their learning progress or project experiences in a short video diary format, using PowerPoint slides for structure. 
B1-Competence:
- Identify at least one learning accomplishment and one difficulty encountered, explaining briefly why they occurred.
- Include a short reflection on how these insights might shape future tasks or goals.
- Maintain clear, understandable language in slides and voiceover, ensuring a logical flow of ideas.
-  Look up basic self-evaluation or reflection techniques to guide the structure.
- Generate prompts or questions to spark deeper self-reflection, then phrase the final statements in a personal and coherent manner.
